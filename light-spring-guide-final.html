What is Light Spring?

Light Spring is one of the three Spring seasons and sits between True Spring and Light Summer on the seasonal flow chart. This season combines the freshness of Spring with a touch of Summer's softness, creating a delicate, luminous appearance.

When you look in the mirror, the first thing you notice is how light your overall coloring is. There's very little contrast between your skin, eyes, and hair, creating a cohesive, delicate appearance with warm, golden undertones.

Your colors are the pastel shades of spring flowers — light, bright, and undeniably warm. These colors enhance your natural glow without overwhelming your soft features.

Your Characteristics

Physical features that define Light Spring

Eyes

Light blue, green, hazel, or light brown with warm undertones. Bright quality with signature Spring sunburst pattern radiating from the pupil.

Skin

Fair to medium with neutral-warm undertones. Golden, peachy, or ivory tones. Gold jewelry flatters more than silver. Often have freckles.

Hair

Light to medium golden blonde, strawberry blonde, light copper, or light golden brown. Warm, golden undertones with natural highlights.

Contrast

Very low contrast between skin, hair, and eyes. Everything exists in a similar light value range with no dramatically dark areas.

Styling Tips

Color Combinations: Mix neighboring hues (yellow + green, pink + coral) or pair light neutrals with bright accents. Always include at least one bright, clear color in your outfit. Avoid all-neutral looks — Light Spring needs color to come alive!
Patterns & Prints: Opt for small florals, polka dots, delicate prints, and watercolor effects. Pattern elements should be small to medium-sized, busy, and playful. Avoid large geometric patterns, high-contrast black and white prints, or stiff angular designs.
Metals & Jewelry: Gold is your best metal — yellow gold and rose gold both work beautifully. Keep metals light and bright with shiny, brushed, or matte finishes. Avoid antiqued, oxidized, or heavy dark bronze pieces. While silver is generally too cool, you may wear very light polished silver if you lean closer to Light Summer.
